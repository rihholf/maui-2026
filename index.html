Good To Know
⌄
Getting Around
You have a BMW 430i Convertible for the week — top down is the only way to drive Maui's coast roads. Fill the tank before heading up Haleakalā; no gas on the mountain. Drive times from Ka'anapali: Lahaina 10 min · Kapalua 15 min · Iao Valley 25 min · Paia 55 min.
Signal & Offline Areas
No cell signal at Haleakalā summit or Nakalele Blowhole. Download offline maps before heading out on those days. This app works fully offline.
Sun & Water
Reef-safe sunscreen is required on Maui — standard sunscreen is banned to protect coral. Reapply often; the Hawaiian sun is stronger than it feels. Stay hydrated — humidity hits different after the Pacific Northwest.
Ocean Safety
Check conditions before entering the water each day. Nakalele Blowhole is a serious hazard when active — stay well back from the opening. Sea turtles rest on Ka'anapali Beach; enjoy from 15 feet away, no touching.
Tipping & Local Customs
Standard tipping: 18–20% at restaurants. Aloha spirit is real — slow down, make eye contact, say thank you. Most beaches have free parking. Locals call it "shave ice," not "shaved ice.
Packing Reminders
Haleakalā summit reaches 30–40°F — bring heavy layers, gloves, and a warm hat even in October. Red-light flashlight for stargazing. Bug spray for the luau evening. Comfortable walking shoes for Tuesday.
